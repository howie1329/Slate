# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Slate serves individual knowledge workers who capture more work than they can reliably finish. They need a calm, private desktop tool that helps them choose a realistic day rather than manage a team or a calendar.

## Product Purpose

Slate is a local-first macOS planner built around a daily commitment budget. It makes the time cost of each task visible before and after commitment, helping a person decide what work realistically fits today. Success means a new user can capture a task, set capacity, and form a first plan without instructions; a returning user understands the day at a glance from the compact menu-bar experience.

## Positioning

A private daily planner that makes the cost of every commitment visible, so one person can choose what realistically fits today.

## Brand Personality

Calm, private, deliberate. Slate should reduce the pressure and noise of task management while giving the user clear, trusted control over their commitments.

## Anti-references

Avoid the density and coordination overhead of a project-management suite, the prescriptive scheduling of a calendar or time-blocking tool, and an AI assistant that takes control without review.

## Design Principles

1. Make the daily trade-off legible at a glance.
2. Keep the user in control: automation proposes; people decide.
3. Favor a focused, quiet workflow over feature breadth.
4. Design for the compact macOS menu-bar window first, with the full app as more room for the same work.
5. Treat local ownership and reliable offline manual planning as first-class product qualities.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet the standard product baseline: keyboard-operable interactions, clear focus states, sufficient color contrast, reduced-motion support, and macOS screen-reader compatibility. No additional requirements are currently specified.

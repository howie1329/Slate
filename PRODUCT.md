# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Slate serves individual knowledge workers who capture more work than they can reliably finish. They use it from a private macOS menu-bar popover while deciding what deserves space in the current day. They do not need a team workspace, a shared project system, or a calendar replacement.

## Product Purpose

Slate is a local-first macOS planner built around a daily commitment budget. It makes the time cost of each task visible before and after commitment, helping one person choose a realistic day, complete what they can, and deliberately adapt when the day changes.

Success means a new user can capture a task, set capacity, and understand a first plan without instructions. A returning user can open the compact menu-bar surface and understand what is committed, what remains, and what needs attention without opening several other applications.

## Positioning

A private daily planner that makes the cost of every commitment visible, so one person can choose what realistically fits today.

## Brand Personality

Calm, private, deliberate. Slate should reduce the pressure and noise of task management while giving the user clear, trusted control over their commitments. It should feel like a quiet desktop utility: familiar enough to disappear into the workflow, intentional enough to make the daily trade-off feel clear.

## Anti-references

Avoid the density and coordination overhead of a project-management suite, the prescriptive scheduling of a calendar or time-blocking tool, and an AI assistant that takes control without review. Avoid productivity gamification, dashboard theater, decorative AI surfaces, and visual complexity that competes with the decision of what fits today.

## Design Principles

1. Make the daily trade-off legible at a glance.
2. Keep the user in control: automation proposes; people decide.
3. Favor a focused, quiet workflow over feature breadth.
4. Design for the compact macOS menu-bar window first, with the full app providing more room for the same work.
5. Treat local ownership and reliable offline manual planning as first-class product qualities.
6. Use hierarchy, spacing, and state changes to communicate importance before adding decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet the standard product baseline: keyboard-operable interactions, clear visible focus states, sufficient color contrast, reduced-motion alternatives, semantic labels, and macOS screen-reader compatibility. Preserve readable text in both light and dark themes. Do not rely on color alone for capacity, completion, error, or configured-state communication.
